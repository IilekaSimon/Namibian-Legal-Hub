The Supreme Law of Namibia

The Namibian Constitution is the fundamental law of Namibia, adopted in February 1990. It establishes the framework of the government, defines the fundamental rights of citizens, and embodies the aspirations of the Namibian people.

Key Features of the Constitution

①

Supreme Law

The Constitution is the supreme law of Namibia, and any other law found to be inconsistent with it is void to the extent of the inconsistency.

Learn more →
②

Bill of Rights

Contains an extensive Bill of Rights that protects fundamental human rights and freedoms, including equality, dignity, and freedom from discrimination.

Explore Rights →
③

Separation of Powers

Establishes three separate branches of government: the Executive, Legislature, and Judiciary, with checks and balances between them.

Constitutional Amendments

First Amendment Act 34 of 1998

Came into force on 24 December 1998

This amendment modified provisions related to the first President's term limits and other administrative matters.

Second Amendment Act 7 of 2010

Came into force on 7 May 2010

Introduced significant changes including the addition of Chapter 10A on Anti-Corruption Measures and amendments to various articles.

Third Amendment Act 8 of 2014

Came into force on 13 October 2014

This comprehensive amendment introduced the position of Vice-President, modified the structure of the Presidency, and made changes to numerous constitutional provisions.
